─── 08 · under the hood

one ceiling unit. five subsystems.

Each chosen for a reason — and the hardest of them already proven.

sensing
Infrared + depth. Maps geometry in the dark — it sees the room, not faces.
compute
On-device inference on Google Coral. A SceneScript-class spatial model, no cloud round-trip.
optics
Short-throw additive light, perspective-corrected per surface. Only light renders; black is the absence of projection.
motion
360° precision motor. Aims the throw to any surface and holds registration as the room changes.
latency
Closed-loop see→aim→project at interactive frame-rate — the tracking core proven in v1.
─── 09 · the physics is the moat

the questions you’re already asking.

“but projection washes out in a lit room”

Washout isn’t a failure mode. It’s the physics.

We project additive light — only lit pixels exist. Black is never drawn dark; it’s simply not drawn, so the wall shows through. Content doesn’t cover your room, it appears on it, like digital ink on the surface that’s already there. The thing every projector startup apologizes for is the thing that makes ours disappear.

“why won’t Apple or Google just do this?”

Because we watched them bet the other way.

Our founder spent two years inside Apple’s spatial program. The trillion-dollar roadmaps are committed to the face — the display you wear. Ambient projection is a different architecture, off-strategy for the incumbents. We’re not racing Apple; we’re building the category they chose to skip.

“what’s actually defensible?”

A two-year head start on the only hard part.

The optics are buyable. The system that makes them vanish is not — real-time tracking, per-surface correction, and a calibration-and-surface flywheel that sharpens with every room it maps. A clone has to match the projector and the on-device model and the room data at once.
