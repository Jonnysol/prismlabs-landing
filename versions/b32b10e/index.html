─── 06 · ambient, not aggressive

part of the room, never imposed on it.

design the absence

quiet is the default.

Content appears when it’s needed and dissolves cleanly when it isn’t — giving the surface back. The empty wall matters as much as the active one.

shared, not personal

everyone in the room sees it.

It projects onto real surfaces, so a whole family or team shares one experience. No pairing, no headset, no per-user cost.

it reads the room

geometry, not faces.

On-device scene understanding maps walls, doors and tables to place light — then lets go. The intelligence stays in your space.

─── 09 · the team & why now

Built by the engineers who shipped the last generation of spatial computing — and left to build the next one.

PrismLabs is started by engineers from Snap Spectacles and Meta’s Reality Labs smart-glasses programs, alongside Apple engineers from the Vision Products Group who helped ship Apple Vision Pro and worked inside its special-projects teams.

We spent years building the headset future — and watched people take the hardware off after thirty minutes, every time. So we stopped putting the display on the face and started putting it on the room.

— the PrismLabs team

Snap Spectacles & Meta Reality Labs. Shipped consumer smart glasses at the companies that defined the category.
